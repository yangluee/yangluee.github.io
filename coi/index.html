Child Opportunity Index

The Child Opportunity Index (COI) is a composite measure of neighborhood opportunity for children across the United States, developed by diversitydatakids.org.

What is the Child Opportunity Index?

The Child Opportunity Index (COI) is a composite index developed and maintained by diversitydatakids.org at Brandeis University. It measures the quality of resources and conditions in U.S. neighborhoods that matter for healthy child development — and how equitably those resources are distributed across race, ethnicity, and geography.

Neighborhoods shape the everyday lives of families and children, influencing economic, social, and environmental contexts that drive long-term health, education, and socioeconomic outcomes. Yet not all neighborhoods offer the same opportunities. High levels of residential segregation mean that Black, Hispanic, and American Indian/Alaska Native children disproportionately grow up in neighborhoods with far fewer resources than their White and Asian peers. The COI was built to measure this unequal geography of neighborhood opportunity — and to provide researchers, policymakers, and communities with the tools to address it.

Three Domains, 44 Indicators

COI 3.0 captures neighborhood opportunity through 44 indicators grouped into three domains and 14 subdomains:

Domain Subdomains (examples)
Education Early childhood education, Elementary education, Secondary & post-secondary education, Educational resources
Health & Environment Access to green space, Healthy food access, Environmental health, Housing quality
Social & Economic Economic resources, Employment, Social capital, Socioeconomic inequity, Wealth

For each census tract, the COI produces scores at the overall, domain, and subdomain levels — enabling fine-grained analyses of exactly where and how neighborhoods fall short.

How It Has Evolved

The COI has been continuously improved since its first release:

Version Year Released Indicators Geography Years of Data
COI 1.0 2014 19 Census tract 2012
COI 2.0 2020 29 Census tract 2012, 2017
COI 3.0 2025 44 Census block* 2012–2023

*Only census tract data is publicly available.

COI 3.0 introduces important methodological advances including machine learning–based data quality improvements (Lasso, Ridge Regression, Random Forests), OpenAI embeddings for classifying food retailers, expanded measures for school quality, green space, social capital, housing quality, and wealth, and 14 subdomains for more granular analysis.

Why It Matters

The COI is uniquely suited to capture how structural racism shapes children’s opportunities across multiple dimensions simultaneously — from school quality and environmental health to employment and housing. A single metric like the neighborhood poverty rate cannot capture the compounding harms of living in a neighborhood with poor schools, high pollution, and limited economic opportunity. The COI does.

As of 2025, COI versions 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 have been cited in over 294 peer-reviewed articles on PubMed and 1,770 studies on Google Scholar — making it one of the most widely used neighborhood opportunity measures in public health, sociology, and social policy research.
